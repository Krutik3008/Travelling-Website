Hampi

History

Hampi was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire from the 14th to the 16th century. The empire was one of the most powerful and prosperous in South India, and it left behind a rich legacy of art, culture, and architecture.

Features

  • Hampi has several ancient temples, including the Virupaksha Temple, the Hazara Rama Temple, and the Vittala Temple.
  • The city has several ruins and monuments, including the King's Balance, the Lotus Mahal, and the Elephant Stables.
  • Hampi is surrounded by beautiful landscapes, including the Tungabhadra River, the Anjaneya Hill, and the Matanga Hill.

Visiting Hampi

Hampi can be visited throughout the year, although the best time to visit is from October to February when the weather is cool and pleasant. Visitors can explore the city on foot or by bicycle, or hire a guide to learn more about its history and culture. There are several accommodation options available, including hotels, guesthouses, and homestays.
