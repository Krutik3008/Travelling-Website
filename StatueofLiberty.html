Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty is a colossal neoclassical sculpture located on Liberty Island in New York Harbor, in the United States. It is one of the most recognizable landmarks in the world and a symbol of freedom and democracy.

History

The Statue of Liberty was a gift from France to the United States, and it was dedicated in 1886. The statue was designed by Frédéric-Auguste Bartholdi, and the pedestal was designed by Richard Morris Hunt. It was built to commemorate the centennial of the American Revolution and to symbolize the friendship between France and the United States.

Features

  • The statue is made of copper and stands 305 feet tall from the base to the tip of the torch. It is one of the tallest statues in the world.
  • The statue's pedestal is made of granite and stands 154 feet tall.
  • The statue's torch is made of gold leaf, and it was restored in 1986 as part of the centennial celebration.
  • The statue's crown has seven spikes, representing the seven continents and seven seas of the world.

Visiting the Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty is open to visitors year-round, and visitors can purchase tickets online or on-site. It is recommended to buy tickets in advance to avoid long lines. Visitors can take a ferry to Liberty Island, where they can visit the statue and the museum. Visitors can also climb to the top of the pedestal or the crown for a panoramic view of New York City.
