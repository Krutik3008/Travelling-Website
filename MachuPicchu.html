History

Machu Picchu was built in the 15th century by the Inca Empire, and it was abandoned during the Spanish conquest in the 16th century. The city was rediscovered in 1911 by Hiram Bingham, an American archaeologist, and it has since become a popular tourist attraction.

Features

  • Machu Picchu is situated on a mountain ridge and surrounded by steep cliffs and lush forests.
  • The city is made of stone and includes temples, houses, and terraces.
  • The most famous feature of Machu Picchu is the Intihuatana Stone, which is believed to have been used for astronomical and religious purposes.
  • Machu Picchu also includes the Temple of the Sun, the Temple of the Moon, and the Room of the Three Windows.

Visiting Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu is open to visitors year-round, and visitors can purchase tickets online or on-site. It is recommended to buy tickets in advance to avoid long lines. Visitors can take a train to Aguas Calientes, the nearest town, and then take a bus or hike to Machu Picchu. Visitors can also take guided tours of the city, which provide more information about its history and architecture.
