Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower located on the Champ de Mars in Paris, France. It is one of the most famous landmarks in the world and a symbol of Paris and France.

History

The Eiffel Tower was designed by Gustave Eiffel and built for the 1889 Exposition Universelle, a World's Fair celebrating the centennial of the French Revolution. It was initially intended to be a temporary structure, but it became so popular that it was allowed to remain standing.

Features

  • The tower is 324 meters tall, making it one of the tallest structures in the world.
  • The tower has three levels that can be accessed by stairs or elevators. The top level offers panoramic views of Paris.
  • The tower is made of wrought-iron lattice and painted in shades of brown.
  • The tower is lit up at night, creating a beautiful spectacle that can be seen from far away.

Visiting the Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is open to visitors year-round, and visitors can purchase tickets online or on-site. It is recommended to buy tickets in advance to avoid long lines. Visitors can also enjoy dining at the tower's restaurants, or attend various events and exhibitions that are held there throughout the year.
