Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the Yamuna river in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan, to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal. The Taj Mahal is considered one of the greatest works of Mughal architecture, and is widely regarded as one of the most beautiful buildings in the world.

History

Construction of the Taj Mahal began in 1632 and took 22 years to complete. More than 20,000 workers were employed in its construction. The architect of the Taj Mahal was Ustad Ahmad Lahauri, who was also the chief architect of the Mughal emperor. The Taj Mahal was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983.

Visiting

The Taj Mahal is open to visitors from sunrise to sunset, except on Fridays when it is closed for prayers. Visitors can enter the mausoleum to see the tombs of Shah Jahan and Mumtaz Mahal. Visitors are also allowed to walk around the gardens and take photographs of the exterior of the building. There are also a number of other attractions in Agra, including the Agra Fort and the Fatehpur Sikri.
